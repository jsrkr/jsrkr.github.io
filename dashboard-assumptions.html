Dashboard Notes

Assumptions

The scenario simulator is intentionally transparent. The user changes assumptions, and the charts and maps respond immediately.

Screen-leisure growth

Assumes additional screen leisure increasingly crowds out in-person social life, partner search, couple time, or unstructured away-from-home activity.

Digital-social growth

Assumes some digital interaction preserves or complements union formation rather than simply replacing it.

Remote-work share growth

Assumes the extra home-based margin comes from work and commute savings rather than only from passive digital leisure.

In-person social change

Allows the user to decide whether in-person social time is declining sharply, declining mildly, flat, or recovering.

Gendered-care penalty

Allows remote work to become less fertility-supportive if home-based work also increases unpaid care burdens, especially for women.

What the scenarios are not

They are not causal estimates, not welfare statements, and not forecasts conditioned on a validated structural model.
